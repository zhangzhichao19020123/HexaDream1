Alleviating Janus Problem in Text-to-3D Generation with Diffusion and GAN

Abstract

We presents a novel approach to addressing the multi-headed phenomenon (Janus problem) in text-guided 3D generation. Through the integration of HexaDiffusion, MultiGAN, and Polar3D modules, the method enhances constraints on uncommon viewpoints and improves the consistency of generated images. Experimental results demonstrate that HexaDream significantly improves 3D generation quality across multiple viewpoints, effectively mitigating the Janus problem.

Method

To tackle these challenge, the paper proposes the HexaDream framework, which consists of three key modules: HexaDiffusion, MultiGAN, and Polar3D. Each module addresses specific issues such as insensitivity to uncommon viewpoints, insufficient supervision of rendered images from these viewpoints, and adaptation of real and generated data to novel perspectives, particularly at the top and bottom views respectively. Specifically: 1. HexaDiffusion improves the diffusion model to generate consistent multi-view images. 2. MultiGAN integrates multiple GAN models with HexaDiffusion to enhance the detail and global consistency of the generated images. 3. Polar3D introduces a novel viewpoint sampling strategy to strengthen constraints on top and bottom views, thereby mitigating the multi-headed phenomenon. Experimental results demonstrate that the HexaDream framework significantly outperforms existing methods in alleviating the Janus problem, producing 3D objects with higher consistency and accuracy across multiple viewpoints.
